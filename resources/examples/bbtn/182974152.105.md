## From The Designer

![Left (wrong) an error message that does not state what caused the error or how to fix it. Right (correct) an error message that provides a small guide to remedy the issue.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/a252ab50-b459-44d8-964e-11c2ab772ed7_2160x1080.png)

**Write clear error messages.** An error should help, not merely frighten.

❌ “Form error. Try again.”\
✅ “The password is too short. Use at least 8 characters.”

👉 The user will understand exactly what is wrong.\
👉 They will reach a successful submission faster.

A good error message is a concise instruction, not a cry in the dark.

## What’s Happening to Open‑Source Libraries for the Web

![Meme](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/533223c0-5979-4f19-b8df-4845df26f565_800x533.webp)
*What will happen to open‑source development in the age of coding AI agents?*

If you didn’t miss the mention in the *[In a Nutshell](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/i/182974152/telegraficky)* section about [75% of engineers recently laid off](https://devclass.com/2026/01/08/tailwind-labs-lays-off-75-percent-of-its-engineers-thanks-to-brutal-impact-of-ai/) by *Tailwind Labs*, you’ll sense that something is up with open‑source software. Tailwind’s crisis highlights the fragility of open‑source in the AI age. AI coding agents happily consume open‑source — but they bypass premium paid services that made open‑source development sustainable. The Tailwind case shows how problematic that model can be.

Tailwind Labs let go of three‑quarters of its engineers after revenues fell by eighty per cent — despite record usage of their popular CSS framework. The reason? The main users of Tailwind are no longer humans but coding AI agents. These agents can generate front‑end code at scale without paying. They don’t need support, [don’t buy UI components or consultancy](https://tailwindcss.com/plus). That undermines business models based on paid services.

Tailwind CSS is a free open‑source tool. The company supported it through add‑ons and premium products. But Copilot, Replit, Claude Code, Cursor and other AI‑driven tools use it without any feedback or revenue.

Vercel CEO *Guillermo Rauch* pledged to [support further development of Tailwind financially](https://x.com/rauchg/status/2009336725043335338). Yet the question remains: if machines are the primary users of open‑source, who pays for it?

Proposals for paid APIs or limiting robot access are emerging. But that could undermine the very principle of open‑source. [The developer community faces a fundamental challenge](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7415115103953477632/): adapt to an era where users are machines, or gradually fade away. That would be a loss for us all.

## Linkodrome

**Ideas are cheap, execution is ~~also cheap~~ cheap too.** It used to be that ideas were cheap and execution was everything. LLM tools have upended that old truth. The decisive [skill is no longer knowing how to code, but iterating quickly](https://davekiss.com/blog/ideas-are-cheap-execution-is-cheaper/), having taste, choosing the right problem and reaching the right audience. Judgement and the ability to decide what’s worth building become most valuable, says *Dave Kiss*. So not that much changes after all.

**You can’t design software you don’t use.** We don’t entirely agree: you don’t need to have cancer to search for a cure. Fortunately *Sean Goedecke* meant it differently: meaningful software design is possible only when the designer is actively involved in the development of the system. Without direct responsibility for implementation, design loses meaning and impact. That makes sense to us.

**How to approach your career in 2026?** *Vitaly Friedman* offers a year‑long retrospective for designers. He emphasises lateral moves over blind climbs up a ladder and [urges designers to actively reshape their roles](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/product-designers-career-paths-vitaly-friedman-ahedf/). He sees AI as an opportunity to deepen human approaches rather than replace them. Clarity about where we want to move is key to success.

**How design is changing in the age of AI,** explains one of the first model designers *Barron Webster* from Figma. He reveals [why designing AI‑first products is fundamentally different](https://www.aidesignfieldguide.com/articles/barron-webster) – instead of traditional UI, designers must dive into model behaviour, evaluations and prompts. The *[AI Design Field Guide](https://www.aidesignfieldguide.com/)* already hosts articles by *Nate Parotta* (Anthropic), *Amelie Wattenberger* (SHV) and *Maddie Simens* (OpenAI).

**Research is not enough, you must understand the business,** says *Michala Pítrová* in an interview with *Petra Spilková*: [quality research must reflect commercial reality](https://medium.com/design-kisk/jak-si-z%C3%ADskat-d%C5%AFv%C4%9Bru-respondenta-7c183395db0f). Combining humanity with analytical thinking is the path to delivering outputs with concrete impact.

**Choosing the right qualitative UX research method** begins with defining scope, intent and goals. *Allison Grayce Marshall* offers an [overview of methods](https://www.notably.ai/blog/how-to-choose-the-right-qualitative-research-methods) and recommends considering multiple approaches. Start by formulating research questions and finish with proper data analysis and translating insights into design.

> “Your 10 years design experience means ’nothing’ in 2026”\
> **— [Guidione Machava](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7415289981981929472/)**

**How to design a website.** On *Perpetual Education* they flipped the classic *[Elements of User Experience](https://web.archive.org/web/20041128155455/http://jjg.net/elements/pdf/elements.pdf)* diagram by *[Jesse James Garrett](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/jesse-james-garrett)* upside down and produced a [practical checklist](https://perpetual.education/how-to-design-a-website/). Real design should serve purpose, not impression. A website is a means, not the end. Good products arise when form follows function — starting with strategy.

**RSS is not a relic,** but core infrastructure of the modern web. It enables direct connection between publisher and reader without algorithmic or platform interference. [RSS powers not only blogs and podcasts but also news apps and enterprise services.](https://werd.io/why-rss-matters/) In an era of platform lockdown, RSS needs protection and rethinking. Its simplicity, openness and reliability are key to a sustainable and free online space.

**The formula that will change how you think about design.** Psychologist *Kurt Lewin* defined behaviour in the 1930s as a function of person and environment `(B = f(P, E))`. This formula profoundly influenced design thinking: [the designer’s task is not to create artefacts but to design behaviour](https://eleganthack.com/the-formula-that-changed-how-i-think-about-design/). According to *[Christina Wodtke](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/christina-wodtke)*, designers have only two tools: understanding users (through research) and shaping the environment (apps, web, physical spaces).

*Kevin Chen* created **Font Fish, an interactive map of typefaces**, where proximity indicates visual similarity. Using neural networks and 2D projections it helps quickly [find alternatives to a chosen font](https://font.fish/).

## Featured on ŘSoW

[Aaron Swartz](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/aaron-swartz) saw the web as a tool of power with ethical consequences. Sharing knowledge was for him a moral duty rather than a legal question. He criticised paywalls on public research as unfair and reinforcing inequality.

[Web Check](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/web-check) is a tool that reveals what public information your website broadcasts. It scans DNS, TLS, HTTP headers, redirects and technology footprints, flagging common configuration weaknesses.

[Minimum Lovable Product](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/mlp) (MLP) is the smallest meaningful version of a digital product that not only works but creates a positive relationship with the user on first contact. Unlike the often‑misunderstood [MVP](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/mvp) it doesn’t excuse unfinished interfaces but emphasises clarity, care and quality.

[Moodboard](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/moodboard) is useful for aligning the visual and emotional direction of a digital product before crafting concrete interfaces. It helps turn vague notions like trust or clarity into a shared visual language.
