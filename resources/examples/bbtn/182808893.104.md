## From The Designer

![Left (wrong) a placeholder inside a form field instead of a label. Right (correct) a label for the form field.](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/69b678a6-f7f2-4eac-8dc3-ea2ee8ddb9b2_2160x1080.png)

**Don’t hide labels in placeholders.** A placeholder is not a substitute for a label.

❌ The label disappears the moment I start typing\
✅ Visible label above the field + supplemental placeholder

👉 The user knows what belongs in the field even while typing.\
👉 The number of input errors is reduced.

A visible label for a form field saves memory and prevents mistakes.

## What’s in Store This Year

We tried to gaze into the crystal ball and predict the trends for this year that will affect online business, e‑commerce and SEO:

- Mass adoption of zero‑click purchases – via search, AI assistants, without needing to visit the website.
- Normalisation of social commerce – TikTok Shop, Instagram Checkout, YouTube storefronts in the EU.
- Voice shopping and voice interactions as primary UX in mobile apps and smart assistants.
- Full dominance of AI‑generated answers in search (especially Google and Bing). Decreasing relevance of metadata, increasing importance of brand mentions, authority and citations.
- Expansion of structured data for content: entities, topical clusters, schemas (schema.org), LLM optimisation.
- Emergence of autonomous agents in online services (orders, account management, troubleshooting).
- Greater emphasis on accessibility‑by‑default due to legal risk (especially in the EU and US).
- Increased interest in headless CMS and composable architecture for performance and agility.
- Development of legal frameworks around digital likenesses, deepfakes and AI avatars.
- Bans on real‑time facial recognition in the Czech Republic, France and other EU countries.
- Strengthening of user rights: digital rights, data access, the right to an explanation of AI decisions.

We’ll see whether we got it right when we look back at the end of 2026. Besides trends, a legislative storm is heading our way, mostly originating from the European Commission and Parliament. We reviewed the legislative process for this year:

- Full enforcement of the [Cybersecurity Act](https://www.zakonyprolidi.cz/cs/2025-264)
- Amendment to the Competition Act (introducing personal liability of company executives for cartels)
- Mandatory [„Withdraw from contract” button](https://retailnews.cz/2025/11/19/tlacitko-odstoupit-od-smlouvy-revoluce-v-online-obchodovani/) for online transactions from 19 June.
- Adoption of European rules for [work permits and employee cards](https://www.mpsv.cz/pro-cizince) (important for startups hiring digital nomads and foreigners)
- Proposal for the [Cloud and AI Development Act](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/thinktank/en/document/EPRS_BRI(2025)779251) (aimed at supporting European infrastructure and setting standards for cloud services)
- [Review of the Digital Markets Act](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/consultation-first-review-digital-markets-act-2025-07-03_en#:~:text=Commission%20is%20required%20to%C2%A0conduct%20a%20review%20of%20the%20DMA%20by%203%20May%202026) (possible extension of scope)
- Full application of the [AI Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj/eng) (2 August, obligations for documentation for personalisation, AI scoring in e‑commerce, etc.)
- [Digital Fairness Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Fairness_Act) (limits on manipulative interfaces: [dark patterns](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/dark-patterns), transparency in advertising and recommender systems)

## Linkodrome

**Evo Design System from eBay won the Design System Awards 2025.** The [eBay Playbook design system](https://playbook.ebay.com/) was recognised by the professional community for its clarity and inspiring approach. Other [awarded design systems](https://zeroheight.com/blog/celebrating-the-2025-design-system-awards-winners/) included Dice (Entain & BetMGM), DDS (Tesco), PIE (Just Eat Takeaway), pAIella (Thomson Reuters) and Rise Design System (Nutrien Ag Solutions).

**The research phase is a myth.** Instead of asking how many interviews or A/B tests are enough, we should ask what value they deliver and when to ramp them up or scale them down. *Adrian Howard* criticises the phase‑based approach to research and design, proposing a signal‑driven approach: [research and testing are processes to be continuously tuned](https://adrianhoward.com/posts/turn-it-up-turn-it-down/), not one‑off activities.

*Search without unnecessary dogs. ([@historyinmemes](https://x.com/historyinmemes/status/1940320538574168196))*

**Without including users, design is not human‑centred.** *Jared M. Spool* criticises the practice of designers and developers building products without deeper understanding of the people they design for. Instead of real users we rely on mediated expert opinions or synthetic personas generated by AI. We cannot describe a single concrete user’s day. Without that knowledge [our products are mere guesses](https://articles.centercentre.com/its-time-we-seriously-talk-about-users-and-experiences/).

**How to build an effective UX research repository in Notion:** *Odette Jansen* details how she [built her UX research repository in Notion](https://odettejansen.medium.com/utilising-notion-as-your-ux-research-repository-005c7dc36726). She highlights benefits such as accessibility, efficiency and knowledge preservation, and advises on setting taxonomy with filters, tags and structure.

**Static files instead of a CMS.** *Lee Robinson* describes how he completely [migrated a site from a headless CMS to plain code and Markdown](https://leerob.com/agents) in three days using AI agents, for just $260. The result is faster development, lower costs and elimination of unnecessary technical complexity.

**ASCII is the new Balsamiq.** *Carlos Chinchilla* introduces [ASCII‑Driven Development](https://medium.com/@calufa/ascii-driven-development-850f66661351) as a tool for rapid, cheap UI prototyping. The approach separates structure from aesthetics, accelerating the path from idea to working product, and counters the trend of jumping straight to high‑fidelity designs.

**Design is more than code.** *Karri Saarinen* argues that good [design starts with finding the right problem](https://linear.app/now/design-is-more-than-code), not with immediately solving it. He warns that obsession with speed and output can squeeze out the conceptual phase that gives work meaning.

**Don Norman calls for an evolution of design.** At 90, *[Don Norman](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/don-norman)* advocates moving from *Human‑Centred Design* to a more systemic *Humanity‑Centred Design* (HCD+). Design should be seen as a tool for social change, not merely aesthetic improvement (see [what I learned from Don Norman](https://uxdesign.cc/from-hcd-to-hcd-what-i-learned-from-don-norman-24a3cdf79c4c)).

**How to design for deaf people.** Hearing loss takes many forms, causes and cultural contexts. [Simply adding captions is not enough,](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2025/12/how-design-for-with-deaf-people/) says *Vitaly Friedman*. Design must account for user diversity: not everyone knows sign language, reads written text easily, or can lip‑read. It helps to offer multiple forms of communication, involve the community itself and avoid assumptions.

**The last days of social networks.** Social media, once promising connection and authenticity, is now flooded with mindless, algorithmically optimised content often generated by AI. Human interaction gives way to robotic noise and an attention struggle. Users are retreating to smaller, more private communities. [Social media as we know it is ending.](https://www.noemamag.com/the-last-days-of-social-media/)

## Featured on ŘSoW

[Christina Wodtke](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/christina-wodtke) is known in UX for pushing human‑centred design towards designing systems with long‑term consequences. She emphasises ethics, information architecture and design’s responsibility for human behaviour. 

[QuickLeap.io](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/quickleapio) is a rule‑driven web request redirect service. It enables conditional routing without owning infrastructure while preserving full URLs for SEO and analytics. 

[See–Think–Do–Care](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/stdc) (STDC) is a digital strategy framework based on user intent. It helps to design and evaluate websites and products according to real human needs, not only conversions.

[Dark patterns](https://www.reknisioweb.cz/p/dark-patterns) are UI design practices that manipulate user decision‑making and undermine informed consent. They are evaluated by effect rather than intent and are subject to regulation.
